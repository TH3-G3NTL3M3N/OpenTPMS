Enclosure Sealing Approach

Must be: waterproof, sealant-resistant, AND user-openable for battery swap. All fit within ~16mm x 30mm footprint.

A

Twist-Lock with O-Ring

Two-piece enclosure with a quarter-turn twist-lock lid. O-ring gasket provides IP67+ seal. User twists open, swaps CR1225, twists shut.

Pros

  • Proven sealing method (watch backs, dive lights)
  • Easy to open/close without tools
  • O-ring is replaceable too

Cons

  • Twist mechanism adds ~1-2mm height
  • More complex injection mold (undercuts)
  • Risk of sealant gunking up the twist channel
B

Snap-Fit Lid with O-Ring

Main body + snap-on lid with O-ring groove. Open with a coin or thumbnail in a pry slot. Snap provides retention, O-ring provides seal.

Pros

  • Thinner profile — snap adds minimal height
  • Simpler mold than twist-lock
  • Familiar design (TV remotes, small electronics)

Cons

  • Snap tabs can fatigue/break over many cycles
  • Slightly less positive seal feel than twist
  • Needs a pry point — could let sealant in if not designed well
C

Screw-Down Lid with O-Ring

Tiny stainless screws (2x M1.2) hold a lid plate down onto an O-ring. User opens with a small screwdriver or Allen key. Most positive seal.

Pros

  • Best seal — even pressure on O-ring
  • Simple mold geometry (no undercuts)
  • Very durable, unlimited open/close cycles

Cons

  • Requires a tool to open (tiny screwdriver)
  • Screws could corrode or strip over time
  • Adds ~1g for metal screws + threaded inserts
